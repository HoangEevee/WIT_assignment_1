ABOUT DIABETES

Diabetes is a serious and complex health condition that can significantly affect one's quality of life.
This is why it is important to learn about the condition to effectively manage it.
Hopefully, this page will give a quick run down on diabetes.

WHAT IS DIABETES?

Diabetes is a complex health condition, where the body has very high sugar levels. Normally, the pancreas produces insulin, a hormone that helps regulates the glucose (or sugar) in your body and converts it into usable energy.

There are 3 main types of diabetes.

  • Type 1 diabetes - wherein the body's immune system destroys the cells in the pancreas that produces the insulin.
  • Type 2 diabetes - wherein the body no longer produces enough insulin or the body slowly resists the insulin being produced.
  • Gestational diabetes - occurs during pregnacy, usually disappears when the pregnancy ends.
  • Pre-diabetes - the surprise 4th type, where the body blood sugar levels are higher than normal, but not high enough to be diagnosed as diabetes.

WHAT ARE THE SYMPTOMS?

The following are some common symptoms that people with diabetes experiences, and is not replaceable with a medical diagnosis. Please consult a medical professional if you feel unwell.

Type 1 diabetes is usually spotted early due to its sudden symptoms. Type 2 diabetes usually exhibits no symptoms, or ones that go unnoticed for a long time.

  • Excessive thirst or hunger
  • Passing more urine than usual
  • Feeling tired and lethargic
  • Unexplained weight loss (for type 1)
  • Gradual weight gain (for type 2)
  • Having cuts that heal slowly
  • Itchy skin or skin infections
  • Blurred vision

CAN DIABETES BE PREVENTED?

Unfortunately, there are currently no preventative methods against type 1 diabetes.

However, there is evidence that maintaining healthy living habits can delay or even prevent type 2 diabetes.

Some of these include

  1. Maintaining a healthy weight
  2. Regular physical activity
  3. Making healthy food choices
  4. Managing blood pressure
  5. Managing cholesterol levels
  6. Not smoking
