ABOUT DIABETES

Diabetes is a serious and complex health condition that can significantly affect one's quality of life.
In order to effectively manage diabetes, it is important to understand the condition.
Please read further for a quick run down on diabetes.

WHAT IS DIABETES?

Diabetes is a chronic health condition where the body loses its ability to produce insulin and can no longer regulate the body's glucose levels. This high glucose level (hyerglycaemia) can cause damage to the eyes, kidneys and nerves over time. Other dangers inclue heart disease and strokes.

There are 3 main types of diabetes:

  • Type 1 diabetes - where the body's immune system destroys the cells in the pancreas that produces the insulin.
  • Type 2 diabetes - where the body no longer produces enough insulin or the body slowly resists the insulin being produced.
  • Gestational diabetes - occurs during pregnacy, usually disappears when the pregnancy ends.
  • Pre-diabetes - the surprise 4th type, where the body blood sugar levels are higher than normal, but not high enough to be diagnosed as diabetes.

WHAT ARE THE SYMPTOMS?

The following are some common symptoms that people with diabetes experiences, and is not replaceable with a medical diagnosis. Please consult a medical professional if you feel unwell.

Type 1 diabetes is usually spotted early due to its sudden symptoms. Type 2 diabetes usually exhibits no symptoms, or ones that go unnoticed for a long time.

  • Excessive thirst or hunger
  • Passing more urine than usual
  • Feeling tired and lethargic
  • Unexplained weight loss (for type 1)
  • Gradual weight gain (for type 2)
  • Having cuts that heal slowly
  • Itchy skin or skin infections
  • Blurred vision

CAN DIABETES BE PREVENTED?

Unfortunately, there are currently no preventative methods against type 1 diabetes. However, there is evidence that maintaining healthy living habits can delay, or even prevent, type 2 diabetes.

Some methods of preventing type 2 diabetes include:

  • Maintaining a healthy weight
  • Regular physical activity
  • Making healthy food choices
  • Managing blood pressure
  • Managing cholesterol levels
  • Not smoking
